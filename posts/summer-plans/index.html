Planning for Summer '22

Getting the most out of my summer.

NOTE: Because I am writing this entry after summer ended, this will be a more “reflection” piece than a “planning” one.

Going into the Summer#

This summer was all about FOCUS. Now that I finished sophomore year and I knew that Computer Science was indeed for me, it was time to FOCUS on a specific technical area I wanted to develop my skills in. But for me, it was pretty straight forward.

Without realizing it, my world was surrounded by data. The financial spreadsheets I made for my high school robotics club, the soccer player statistic reports, to artificial intelligence projects recognizing handwritten digits. And especially after working at Saudi Aramco last summer and accepting a position at Pfizer this summer working in Big Data and Artificial Intelligence, I felt as if I was destined to work in Data Science. So my plan this summer was to study subjects related to Data Science while also preparing for a technical or finance company internship for Summer 2023.

Initial Summer Plans#

Given my plans to get further into data science, I made three obligations to myself (I like working in threes) at the beginning of the summer:

Business Analyst Internship @ Pfizer#

Definitely the most straightforward one of the three: to complete my internship at Pfizer. Not only that, but to complete my internship with dignity by going above and beyond the call of my work while also meeting and learning about as many of my colleagues as possible during my short time at Pfizer. However, I did not want to create an environment for myself where I was overstepping my bounds as an intern and not go beyond the allocated 8 hours a day, 5 days a week required of me.

My projects during my time at Pfizer was create a dashboard through Spotfire of the inbound and outbound messages of the Clinical Supply Distribution Center Data, understand and present information about Microsoft’s AI Builder and review the onboarding documents of the CSDS User Documentation System.

Google Data Analytics Certificate#

The Google Data Analytics Certificate is a professional analytics course offered by Google teaching students about analyzing and processing data to gain key business insights. As someone who wants to work in data, I wanted to take this certificate to get to know the data analysis process, from asking a question, preparing and processing the data, analyzing the data, and sharing and acting on it, and eventually applying it to real life data analysis situations later in my career. I planned to earn my certificate by the end of the summer (6 months into 3 months)

Technical Interview Prep#

As for Technical Interview Prep, I decided to enroll in a Technical Interview Prep course through CodePath to keep myself in check throughout the summer. I had taken a Data Structures & Algorithms class before, but we only covered up to obscure trees and not enough to cover graphs. In addition, I didn’t really know how to apply those concepts into technical interviews. After taking a brief coding assessment (luckily the most difficult problem was traversing a tree: something I knew how to do), I was placed into the 12-week long Advanced Class.

While my internship at Pfizer had a hard deadline at the end of August and I feasibly had an end goal for my Data Analytics Certificate, my technical interview prep journey was different. Although I did earn a certificate by the end of the Interview Prep Course, it was not like I would be prepared by the course itself, I had to prepare.

So before the class started around early June, I took a CodeAcademy Course I had with my Pro Subscription (from Fall 2021) to get brushed up on the data structures and algorithms covered in the technical interview prep course, and hopefully getting some tips through the course.

Plans Evolving over the Summer#

If there is any guarantee in life, it’s that there is no guarantee in anything. As such, my plans molded over time.

Business Analyst Internship @ Pfizer#

Through keeping my goal to be diligent in my work at Pfizer, I received more complications in my work over time. While my inbound and outbound message dashboard was completed when I finished it, my other two projects had additional complexity added onto their initial assignments.

The onboarding documentation complexity was simple: outside of the documentation, I gave suggestions on what to fix of the UI of the CSDS System. Since the system dates back to the late 2000s, I suggested that some UI elements be placed in certain locations to be easier to find while also suggesting changing the formatting and wording of the page to be more clear.

But my largest complexity was the AI Builder project. While my original assignment was to just make a presentation on AI Builder’s capabilities, I decided to take on a task of applying AI Builder to a real use case at Pfizer. Sitting down in one of the GCS-AI meetings at Pfizer with the COE of AI at Pfizer, I realized a use case when someone suggested using a AWS service to process invoice documents and the viability of it. However, I brought up how my current project in AI Builder was perfect since the service did not require extensive coding or data science training to access the power of text extraction and automation through Power Automate.

So I decided to demonstrate a use case of AI Builder through training and creating a model flow of my own that could easily be replicated by anyone in the Pfizer Network. I also led training sessions for people to learn AI Builder and Power Automate and created a training document for future employees to refer to if they wanted to harness AI Builder’s power for themselves.

Google Data Analytics Certificate#

To the surprise of myself, I was able to finish the Google Data Analytics Certificate quicker than I anticipated. While I was planning to finish by the end of the summer (around now), I managed to complete it by the middle of July. Although it was incredibly insightful what the data analysis process was like at Google, I also decided that I needed to improve my data science skills through other means.

Technical Interview Prep#

I realized early on that the course itself was not going to help me improve my technical prep skills. While the course was focused on algorithmic skills, as a data scientist as my mentor suggested, I needed to improve my SQL and math / statistics knowledge as well.

Additional Work#

Closing Remarks#

At the end, I was able to complete my internship @ Pfizer as a Business Analyst, the Google Data Analytics Certificate, and CodePath’s Advanced Technical Prep Course. In addition, I am going to continue to polish my data science skills through data analytics / machine learning projects while also preparing for data science interviews through algorithmic, SQL, and statistical math preperation.

However, my journey does not stop there as a growing data scientist and learner. I hope through these blogs I can not only appreciate the process I make along the way, but also inspire others to write their own progress blogs as well.
