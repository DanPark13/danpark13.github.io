## Going into the Summer

This summer was all about **FOCUS**. Now that I finished sophomore year and I knew that Computer Science was indeed for me, it was time to **FOCUS** on a specific technical area I wanted to develop my skills in. But for me, it was pretty straight forward.

Without realizing it, my world was surrounded by data. The financial spreadsheets I made for my high school robotics club, the soccer player statistic reports, to artificial intelligence projects recognizing handwritten digits. And especially after working at Saudi Aramco last summer and accepting a position at Pfizer this summer working in Big Data and Artificial Intelligence, I felt as if I was destined to work in **Data Science**. So my plan this summer was to study subjects related to Data Science.

## Initial Plans

Given my plans to get further into data science, I made three obligations to myself (I like working in threes) at the beginning of the summer:

### Business Analyst Internship @ Pfizer

Definitely the most straightforward one of the three: to complete my internship at Pfizer. But not only that, but to complete my internship with dignity by going above and beyond the call of my work while also meeting and learning about as many of my colleagues as possible during my short time at Pfizer. 

### Google Data Analytics Certificate

The [Google Data Analytics Certificate](https://grow.google/certificates/data-analytics/) is a professional analytics course offered by Google teaching students about analyzing and processing data to gain key business insights. As someone who wants to work in data, I wanted to take this certificate to get to know the data analysis process, from asking a question, preparing and processing the data, analyzing the data, and sharing and acting on it, and eventually applying it to real life data analysis situations later in my career. I planned to earn my certificate by the end of the summer (6 months into 3 months)

### Technical Interview Prep

As for Technical Interview Prep, I decided to enroll in a Technical Interview Prep course through [CodePath](https://www.codepath.org/courses/tech-interview-prep) to keep myself in check throughout the summer. I had taken a Data Structures & Algorithms class before, but we only covered up to obscure trees and not enough to cover graphs. In addition, I didn't really know how to apply those concepts into technical interviews. After taking a brief coding assessment (luckily the most difficult problem was traversing a tree: something I knew how to do), I was placed into the 12-week long Advanced Class.

While my internship at Pfizer had a hard deadline at the end of August and I feasibly had an end goal for my Data Analytics Certificate, my technical interview prep journey was different. Although I did earn a certificate by the end of the Interview Prep Course, it was not like I would be prepared by the course itself, I had to prepare.

So before the class started around early June, I took a [CodeAcademy Course](https://www.codecademy.com/learn/paths/pass-the-technical-interview-with-python) I had with my Pro Subscription (from Fall 2021) to get brushed up on the data structures and algorithms covered in the technical interview prep course, and hopefully getting some tips through the course.
